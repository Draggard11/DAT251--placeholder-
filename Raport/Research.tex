The paper discusses the idea of how good or bad research strategy is dependent on what the criteria is. It brings up a lot of metaphors such as: a biologist is interested in studying the behavior of an elephant, while a scientist is interested in studying how the parts of the elephant are built. This directly ties to the second part of the paper. The ABC part conforms to the actors, behavior and context. It specifies how you can not satisfy all of these components. The Paper introduces two axes, this then creates 4 areas, and inside those they define 8 research strategies.

The Design Paper distinguishes between Design science and Behavioral science as two research paradigms in information systems. Behavioral science tries to understand and explain through theory and empirical testing, while design science builds artifacts that solve relevant organizational problems. This dual structure appears ideal, behavioral research provides theoretical understanding of problems, and design science uses that understanding into practical solutions.

However, the ABC paper introduces an important limitation to this ideal structure. It argues that empirical research is always constrained by tradeoffs from three components, Generalizability (Actor), precision and control (Behavior) and realism (Context). No research can maximize all three components at once. This directly affects both paradigms, since behavioral theories require empirical validation, and design artifacts require evaluation.

From this, it shows that empirical research is necessary, but limited. Without empirical research, behavioral theory becomes speculation and design artifacts remain untested ideas. At the same time, the ABC paper discusses that research quality must be judged relative to its chosen criteria and unavoidable tradeoffs. These two papers show that research requires both explanation and construction, while also demanding awareness of the methodological limitations involved in evaluating them.

The three papers highlight the importance of critically evaluating development methods and understanding the context in which they are used. The paper about “design thinking” describes how the approach became adopted across different industries because it said that creativity and user empathy could help solve complex problems. However, the paper also points out several weaknesses, such as unrealistic, limited domain expertise, and focusing more on brainstorming rather than implementation. This shows that design thinking can be useful for collaboration and idea generation, but it is not a concrete solution for complex problems.

The paper “What’s the Evidence for Lean” discusses the empirical evidence behind lean production. It argues that conclusions about lean’s superiority were based on questionable interpretations of data, such as ignoring overtime and difference in factory automation. This demonstrates how important it is to critically analyze research data.

The last paper “On the Effectiveness of Pair Programming” reviews empirical studies and finds that pair programming generally improves code quality but requires more effort. Its effectiveness also depends on factors such as task complexity and how experienced the developer is.
